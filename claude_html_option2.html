About

I am a PhD researcher in the Department of Geography & Environment at Loughborough University, working on Arctic dust emissions and their role in the Earth system. My research combines satellite remote sensing, ground-based field measurement, and atmospheric modelling to constrain dust sources across ice-free surfaces in Greenland and Svalbard.

My work sits at the intersection of aeolian and fluvial geomorphology — investigating how glaciofluvial systems supply sediment to wind, and how that sediment is then mobilised and transported through the atmosphere. Prior to my PhD, I completed an MSc by thesis at Durham University examining bedrock river erosion in the North Pennines.

I hold a 2025 IASC Fellowship (Prince Albert II of Monaco Foundation) and sit on the board of directors of the International Society for Aeolian Research.
